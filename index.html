The Rise of Night Cricket in Salt Lake: A Cultural Shift

RD

Rahul Das

12 April 2026 • 4 Min Read

For decades, cricket in Kolkata was a daytime affair. The maidan was crowded by 6 AM, and the stumps were pulled up before the sunset. But a quiet revolution has taken over the artificial turfs of Salt Lake, Sector V, and New Town.

With the rise of IT parks and extended corporate working hours, the traditional 9-to-5 is a myth for many young professionals. By the time they log off, the sun is long gone. Enter the era of the illuminated box turf.

The 10 PM League

"We used to struggle to get 11 guys together on a Sunday morning," says Arindam, a software developer whose team now plays exclusively at 10 PM on Wednesdays. "Now, we just book a 6v6 slot after dinner. It's cooler, there's no traffic, and the LED lights make it feel like an IPL match."

"The turf isn't just about sports anymore. It's the new corporate watercooler. It's where deals are made and stress is burned off."

The data on SquadUp backs this up. Over 65% of our cricket bookings in the Salt Lake hub happen between 8 PM and midnight. Venue owners have caught on, upgrading their facilities with high-lumen floodlights, better shock-absorbent grass, and even late-night refreshment stands serving hot tea and momos.

What You Need for Night Turf Cricket:

  • High-Visibility Gear: Dark clothes blend into the netting. Wear neon or white.
  • Turf Shoes: Leave the metal spikes at home. Rubber studs provide the best grip on artificial grass and prevent injuries.
  • The Right Ball: Standard hard tennis balls can skid unpredictably under the dew. Opt for a medium-weight rubber ball or a turf-specific synthetic ball.

If you haven't experienced the rush of a night match yet, it's time to gather your squad. And if you don't have a team? Use SquadUp's new "Meet Your Pals" feature to book a single slot in an existing match tonight.
